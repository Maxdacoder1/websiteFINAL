Wine Cork Succulent Magnets

Materials Needed:

  • Wine corks
  • Small succulents
  • Magnets
  • Hot glue gun and glue sticks
  • Drill with a small drill bit or a screwdriver
  • Soil (optional)
  • Knife or saw (for cutting corks, if necessary)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare your wine corks by ensuring they are clean and dry. If you want shorter magnets, carefully cut the corks to your desired length.
  2. Use a drill or screwdriver to carefully hollow out a hole in one end of each cork. The hole should be large enough to plant your succulents.
  3. If using soil, add a small amount into the hole in each cork.
  4. Plant a small succulent into the hole of each cork. Gently press the soil around it to secure it in place, if you've used soil.
  5. Using a hot glue gun, attach a magnet to the back of each cork. Make sure the magnet is strong enough to hold the weight of the cork and the plant.
  6. Allow the glue to dry completely before placing your new wine cork succulent magnets on a refrigerator, magnetic board, or any other metal surface.
